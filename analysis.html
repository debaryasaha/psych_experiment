Statistical Figures

  • T-Test P-Value: 6.359745608603189e-13
  • Correlation Coefficient: 0.28852201186160314
  • Correlation P-Value: 0.009446693759018295

In order for a correlation to be statistically significant, the value has to be less than 0.05. In this case, both the T-Test P-Value and the Correlation P-Value are significantly less than 0.05, hence it is statistically significant. Furthermore, the Correlational Coefficient measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables. The correlation coefficient is approximately 0.23, which indicates a positive correlation between the variables. A correlation coefficient closer to 1 indicates a strong positive correlation, while a value closer to -1 indicates a strong negative correlation. However, a value close to 0 suggests a weak or no correlation.
