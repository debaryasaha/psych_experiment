Independent Variable

The Independent Variable was the type of test given to the participants. One test had the colors matching the words, while the others had mismatched colors and words. For example, the word green would be colored purple.

Dependent Variable

The Dependent Variable is the time it takes for the participant to complete the test. The number of mistakes was also counted.

Population

The Population was all students in Metea Valley High School grades 9 - 12.

Hypothesis

Selective Attention - the processes that allow an individual to select and focus on particular stimuli for further processing while simultaneously suppressing distracting information.

If a group of high school students are given a set of colored words that are the same colors as the word, then students will on average say the words 30% faster when compared to a group that says words that arenâ€™t the same color.

Experimental Setup

Both Random Selection and Random Assignment were used to ensure low experimental and participant bias. The participants were to generate a random number between 0 and 1 (online randomizer). The number 0 would place them in the control group while the number 1 would place them in the experimental group. However, the subject did not know which side of the coin corresponded with each test(Single Blind procedure). The experimental group would receive the test with the mismatched colored words while the control group would receive the test with the correct matched colored words. The subjects were then told to read the color, not the word, and were timed on how long it took them to complete the test(timed in seconds). The number of mistakes they made was also counted. All of this data was then entered into a spreadsheet. None of the participant's personal data was recorded such as name, age, grade level, or race.
